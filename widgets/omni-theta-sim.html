Pinning down a model’s latent ability, one roll at a time

Pick the model’s true general trait θ, then roll to see the model’s response to 10 Omni-MATH items. Our confidence in where θ lies narrows, but Omni’s items share dependence we can’t average away (ρ̂ ≈ 0.0012), so each roll teaches you a little less, and we approach a ceiling equivalent to the information of about 800 truly independent items.

Posterior = prior N(0,1) × likelihood, tempered by 1/[1+(n−1)ρ̂] (ρ̂ ≈ 0.0012) so its precision is the effective information about the true trait. The ceiling reflects Omni-Math’s real item dependence, summarized by ρ̂ — not something these idealized independent rolls generate on their own. θ is the population-defined latent trait (mean 0, sd 1 across models).
