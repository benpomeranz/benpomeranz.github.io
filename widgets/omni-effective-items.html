Omni-Math: information from n items (within-benchmark)

I(n) / I(1) = n1 + (n−1)ρ̂  — information from n Omni-Math items, in units of independent items.
0.0012 ceiling 1/ρ̂ ≈ 833 items

ρ̂ is how much Omni-Math items correlate beyond chance, conditioning on Omni-Math's own latent (math) trait. Its null-calibrated within-benchmark value is ≈ 0.0012 (robust estimate; estimators span 0.0008–0.0019), capping information at 1/ρ̂ ≈ 833 independent items. Omni-Math's 1000 items are then worth ~455 independent items about the math trait — its items are only mildly redundant for their own trait. The derivative f′(n) = (1−ρ̂) / [1 + (n−1)ρ̂]² is how much the next item is worth.
